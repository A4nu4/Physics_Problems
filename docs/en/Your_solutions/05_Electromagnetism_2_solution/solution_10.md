To calculate the speed of light in a diamond, we use the relationship between the speed of light in a vacuum, the speed of light in the material, and the refractive index.



### The Speed of Light Formula
The index of refraction ($n$) is defined as the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) to the speed of light in the medium ($v$):

$$n = \frac{c}{v}$$

Where:
*   **$c$ (Speed of light in vacuum):** $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   **$n$ (Index of refraction for diamond):** $2.42$

---

### 1. Rearrange to Solve for $v$
To find the speed in the diamond, we rearrange the formula:

$$v = \frac{c}{n}$$

---

### 2. Final Calculation
Substitute the known values into the equation:

$$v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}}{2.42}$$

$$v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Final Result:
The speed of light in a diamond is approximately **$1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$**.

This is significantly slower than the speed of light in a vacuum (about **41%** of $c$). This high refractive index is what causes the dramatic bending of light and the characteristic "fire" or "sparkle" seen in diamonds.
