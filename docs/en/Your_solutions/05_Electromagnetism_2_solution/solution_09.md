To determine the angle of refraction as a light ray travels from air into glass, we use **Snell's Law**, which describes how light bends when passing between materials with different refractive indices.

### Given Starting Values:
* **Refractive index of air ($n_1$):** $1.00$
* **Refractive index of glass ($n_2$):** $1.50$
* **Angle of incidence ($\theta_1$):** $30^\circ$

---

### 1. The Snell's Law Formula
Snell's Law is expressed as:

$$n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)$$

Where:
* **$\theta_2$** is the angle of refraction we need to find.

---

### 2. Solve for the Angle of Refraction ($\theta_2$)
First, rearrange the formula to isolate $\sin(\theta_2)$:

$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{n_1 \sin(\theta_1)}{n_2}$$

Substitute the known values:

$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{1.00 \cdot \sin(30^\circ)}{1.50}$$

Since $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:

$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.50} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.3333$$

Now, take the inverse sine ($\arcsin$) to find $\theta_2$:

$$\theta_2 = \arcsin(0.3333)$$
$$\theta_2 \approx 19.47^\circ$$

---

### Final Result:
The angle of refraction is approximately **$19.47^\circ$**.
