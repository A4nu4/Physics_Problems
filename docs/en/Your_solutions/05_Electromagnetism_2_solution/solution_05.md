To analyze this parallel-plate capacitor, we will use the standard geometric and energetic formulas for electrostatics.

### Given Starting Values:
*   **Area ($S$):** $0.02 \text{ m}^2$
*   **Separation ($d$):** $5 \text{ mm} = 0.005 \text{ m}$
*   **Potential Difference ($V$):** $500 \text{ V}$
*   **Permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$):** $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$

---

### 1. Calculate the Capacitance ($C$)
The capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor (assuming a vacuum or air between the plates) depends on its geometry:



$$C = \epsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}$$

$$C = (8.854 \times 10^{-12}) \frac{0.02}{0.005}$$
$$C = 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 4$$
$$C \approx 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \text{ F} \quad (\text{or } 35.4 \text{ pF})$$

---

### 2. Calculate the Stored Energy ($W$)
The energy stored in the electric field of the capacitor is given by:

$$W = \frac{1}{2} C V^2$$

$$W = \frac{1}{2} (3.54 \times 10^{-11}) (500)^2$$
$$W = 0.5 \cdot 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \cdot 250,000$$
$$W \approx 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J} \quad (\text{or } 4.43 \ \mu\text{J})$$

---

### 3. Calculate the Electric Field Intensity ($E$)
In a uniform electric field between two plates, the field intensity is the potential difference divided by the distance:



$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$

$$E = \frac{500}{0.005}$$
$$E = 100,000 \text{ V/m} \quad (\text{or } 100 \text{ kV/m})$$

---

### 4. Calculate the Force of Attraction ($F$)
The plates of the capacitor carry opposite charges ($+Q$ and $-Q$) and therefore attract each other. The force can be calculated using the energy-gradient method or the field method:

$$F = \frac{1}{2} Q E = \frac{1}{2} (CV) E$$
Alternatively, using the formula $F = \frac{\epsilon_0 S V^2}{2 d^2}$:

$$F = \frac{(8.854 \times 10^{-12}) \cdot 0.02 \cdot (500)^2}{2 \cdot (0.005)^2}$$
$$F = \frac{1.7708 \times 10^{-13} \cdot 250,000}{0.00005}$$
$$F \approx 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}$$
