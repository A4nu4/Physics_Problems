To list these types of electromagnetic radiation in order of **increasing wavelength**, we move from the most energetic, high-frequency waves (shortest wavelength) to the least energetic, low-frequency waves (longest wavelength).



### Increasing Wavelength Order:

1.  **Gamma rays** (Shortest wavelength)
2.  **X-rays**
3.  **Ultraviolet**
4.  **Infrared**
5.  **Microwaves**
6.  **Radio waves** (Longest wavelength)

---

### Key Relationships in the Spectrum
Understanding the relationship between wavelength, frequency, and energy is helpful for remembering this order:

*   **Inverse Relationship:** Wavelength ($\lambda$) is inversely proportional to frequency ($f$). As the wavelength increases, the frequency decreases.
*   **Energy:** Energy ($E$) is directly proportional to frequency ($E = hf$). Therefore, **Gamma rays** have the highest energy, while **Radio waves** have the lowest energy.
*   **Visible Light:** If included, visible light would sit between **Ultraviolet** and **Infrared**.
