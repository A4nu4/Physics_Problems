To calculate the magnetic field produced by a small current segment, we use the **Biot-Savart Law**. This law describes the magnetic field generated by a constant electric current at a specific point in space.

### Given Starting Values:
*   **Current ($I$):** $3 \text{ A}$
*   **Length of segment ($\Delta L$):** $0.1 \text{ m}$
*   **Distance to point $P$ ($r$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (since the segment is perpendicular to the line to point $P$)
*   **Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$):** $4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

---

### 1. The Biot-Savart Formula
For a short, straight segment of wire, the magnitude of the magnetic field ($\Delta B$) is given by:

$$\Delta B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I \Delta L \sin(\theta)}{r^2}$$

The term $\frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}$ is a constant equal to exactly $10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$.

---

### 2. Final Calculation
Substitute the values into the formula. Because the segment is perpendicular, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$:

$$\Delta B = (10^{-7}) \frac{(3 \text{ A}) (0.1 \text{ m}) (1)}{(0.2 \text{ m})^2}$$

$$\Delta B = (10^{-7}) \frac{0.3}{0.04}$$

$$\Delta B = (10^{-7}) \cdot 7.5$$

$$\Delta B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$$

---

### Final Result:
The magnetic field at point $P$ is **$7.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$** (or $0.75 \ \mu\text{T}$).
