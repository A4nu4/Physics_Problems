Light with a wavelength of **$550 \text{ nm}$** corresponds to the **green** or **yellow-green** portion of the visible spectrum. This wavelength is located near the peak of the human eye's spectral sensitivity under well-lit conditions.



### 1. Calculate the Frequency ($f$)
To find the frequency, we use the wave equation for light, where the speed of light ($c$) is equal to the product of wavelength ($\lambda$) and frequency ($f$):

$$c = \lambda \cdot f$$

**Given Values:**
*   **Speed of light ($c$):** $\approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** $550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} = 5.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$

---

### 2. Solve for Frequency
Rearrange the formula to isolate $f$:

$$f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$$

Substitute the values:

$$f = \frac{3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}}{5.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}}$$

$$f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$$

---

### Final Result:
*   **Color:** Green / Yellow-Green
*   **Frequency:** Approximately **$5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$** (or $545 \text{ THz}$)
