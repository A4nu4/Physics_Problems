To calculate the electric flux through a spherical surface surrounding a point charge, we use **Gauss's Law**, which relates the net flux through a closed surface to the enclosed charge.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Enclosed Charge ($q_{enc}$):** $+2 \text{ C}$
* **Radius ($r$):** $1 \text{ m}$
* **Permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$):** $\approx 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \text{ C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)$

---

### 1. The Gauss's Law Formula
Gauss's Law states that the total electric flux ($\Phi_E$) through any closed surface is equal to the net charge enclosed by the surface divided by the permittivity of free space:

$$\Phi_E = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0}$$

Notably, the electric flux is **independent of the radius** of the spherical surface, as long as the charge remains enclosed.

---

### 2. Final Calculation
Substitute the given charge into the formula:

$$\Phi_E = \frac{2 \text{ C}}{8.85 \times 10^{-12} \text{ C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)}$$

$$\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$$

---

### Final Result:
The electric flux through the spherical surface is approximately **$2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$**.
