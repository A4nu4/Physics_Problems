To calculate the magnetic torque on a current-carrying loop, we use the relationship between the magnetic moment of the loop and the external magnetic field. When the field is parallel to the plane of the loop, the torque is at its maximum value.



### Given Starting Values:
*   **Dimensions:** $10 \text{ cm} \times 5 \text{ cm}$
*   **Current ($I$):** $2 \text{ A}$
*   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.3 \text{ T}$
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (between the magnetic field and the normal to the loop's plane)

---

### 1. Calculate the Area ($A$)
First, we must convert the dimensions to meters and find the area of the rectangle:
$$A = 0.10 \text{ m} \times 0.05 \text{ m} = 0.005 \text{ m}^2$$

---

### 2. Calculate the Magnetic Moment ($\mu$)
The magnetic dipole moment of the loop is the product of the current and the area:
$$\mu = I \cdot A$$
$$\mu = (2 \text{ A}) \cdot (0.005 \text{ m}^2) = 0.01 \text{ A}\cdot\text{m}^2$$

---

### 3. Calculate the Torque ($\tau$)
The magnitude of the torque is given by the cross product of the magnetic moment and the magnetic field:
$$\tau = \mu \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Since the magnetic field is **parallel to the plane** of the loop, the angle between the field and the **normal vector** (the direction perpendicular to the loop's surface) is $90^\circ$. Because $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$:
$$\tau = (0.01 \text{ A}\cdot\text{m}^2) \cdot (0.3 \text{ T}) \cdot 1$$
$$\tau = 0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$$

---

### Final Result:
The magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop is **$0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$** (or $3 \times 10^{-3} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$).
