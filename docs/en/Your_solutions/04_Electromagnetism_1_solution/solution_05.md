To make a proton levitate, the upward electric force must exactly balance the downward gravitational force acting on it.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Mass of proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Charge of proton ($e$):** $1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Gravitational acceleration ($g$):** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

### 1. Set up the Equilibrium Condition

For the proton to remain stationary (levitate), the net force acting on it must be zero:


$$F_{net} = F_e - F_g = 0$$


$$F_e = F_g$$


Where:
* **$F_e = qE$** (Electric force)
* **$F_g = mg$** (Gravitational force)

---

### 2. Solve for Electric Field Strength ($E$)

Substitute the specific variables for a proton ($q = e$ and $m = m_p$):


$$e \cdot E = m_p \cdot g$$


Now, rearrange the equation to isolate $E$:


$$E = \frac{m_p \cdot g}{e}$$


---

### 3. Calculate the Final Value

Substitute the given numerical values into the equation:


$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \cdot (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$


$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$


$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$


---

### Final Result:
The electric field strength required to levitate a proton is approximately **$1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$**.

Because the proton is positively charged, the direction of this electric field must be **upward** to provide the necessary upward force. Since this field strength is incredibly small, it shows just how much stronger the electromagnetic force is compared to gravity at the subatomic level.
