To calculate the force acting on the particle, we use the magnetic component of the Lorentz force law. When a charged particle moves through a magnetic field, it experiences a force perpendicular to both its velocity and the magnetic field direction.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Charge ($q$):** $2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Mass ($m$):** $4 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5 \text{ T}$
* **Speed ($v$):** $10^6 \text{ m/s}$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (perpendicular)

---

### 1. The Force Formula
The magnitude of the magnetic force ($F_B$) is given by:


$$F_B = q \cdot v \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)$$


Since the particle enters the field perpendicular to the field lines, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$. The equation simplifies to:


$$F_B = q \cdot v \cdot B$$


---

### 2. Final Calculation
Substitute the given numerical values into the simplified equation:


$$F_B = (2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \cdot (10^6 \text{ m/s}) \cdot (0.5 \text{ T})$$


$$F_B = (2 \times 0.5) \times (10^{-19} \cdot 10^6)$$


$$F_B = 1 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$$


---

### Final Result:
The magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle is **$10^{-13} \text{ N}$**.
