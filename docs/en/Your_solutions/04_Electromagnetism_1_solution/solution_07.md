To find the radius of the circular path, we must first determine the velocity the electron gains from the acceleration and then apply the principles of centripetal force in a magnetic field.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Potential Difference ($V$):** $5000 \text{ V}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.1 \text{ T}$
* **Mass of electron ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Charge of electron ($e$):** $1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$

---

### 1. Calculate the Velocity ($v$)
When an electron is accelerated through a potential difference, its gain in kinetic energy is equal to the work done by the electric field ($W = eV$):


$$\frac{1}{2} m_e v^2 = eV$$


Solving for $v$:


$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m_e}}$$


$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 5000}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}}$$


$$v \approx 4.19 \times 10^{7} \text{ m/s}$$


---

### 2. Determine the Radius ($r$)
As the electron enters the magnetic field perpendicularly, the magnetic force ($F_B = evB$) acts as the centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{m_e v^2}{r}$):


$$evB = \frac{m_e v^2}{r}$$


Rearranging to solve for the radius:


$$r = \frac{m_e v}{eB}$$


---

### 3. Final Calculation
Substitute the velocity and the given values into the radius formula:


$$r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \cdot (4.19 \times 10^7)}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 0.1}$$


$$r = \frac{3.82 \times 10^{-23}}{1.6 \times 10^{-20}}$$


$$r = 2.39 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$$


---

### Final Result:
The radius of the circular path is **$2.39 \text{ mm}$** (or $0.00239 \text{ m}$).
