To find the magnitude of the electric and gravitational forces between a proton and an electron in a hydrogen atom, we use **Coulomb's Law** and **Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation**.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$
* **Charge of electron/proton ($e$):** $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Mass of electron ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Mass of proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Coulomb's constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^{9} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
* **Gravitational constant ($G$):** $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

---

### 1. Calculate the Electric Force ($F_e$)

The electric force is an attractive force between the opposite charges:


$$F_e = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$


$$F_e = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{(1.602 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$


$$F_e \approx 8.21 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$$


---

### 2. Calculate the Gravitational Force ($F_g$)

The gravitational force is an attractive force between the masses:


$$F_g = G \frac{m_e m_p}{r^2}$$


$$F_g = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$


$$F_g \approx 3.62 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$$




---

### 3. Calculate the Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)

The ratio compares the relative strengths of these two fundamental forces. Note that since both formulas depend on $1/r^2$, the distance cancels out, making this ratio a constant for these two particles:


$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{k e^2}{G m_e m_p}$$


$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{8.21 \times 10^{-8}}{3.62 \times 10^{-47}}$$


$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}$$


---

### Final Result:
* **Electric Force ($F_e$):** $8.21 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$
* **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** $3.62 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$
* **Ratio ($F_e / F_g$):** $2.27 \times 10^{39}$

This result demonstrates that on an atomic scale, the electric force is unimaginably stronger than the gravitational force (by 39 orders of magnitude). Gravity is essentially negligible when dealing with subatomic interactions.
