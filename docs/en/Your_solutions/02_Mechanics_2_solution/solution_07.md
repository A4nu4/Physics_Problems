To solve for the acceleration of the 10 kg block, we identify all the horizontal forces acting on it while accounting for the friction at both the top and bottom surfaces.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Mass of top block ($m_1$):** $5 \text{ kg}$
* **Mass of bottom block ($m_2$):** $10 \text{ kg}$
* **Applied horizontal force ($F$):** $45 \text{ N}$
* **Coefficient of kinetic friction ($\mu_k$):** $0.2$
* **Acceleration due to gravity ($g$):** $9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

### 1. Identify the Normal Forces

We must determine the normal force at each interface to calculate the friction.

* **At the top surface ($N_1$):** The 10 kg block supports the weight of the 5 kg block.


$$N_1 = m_1 \cdot g$$


$$N_1 = 5 \cdot 9.81 = 49.05 \text{ N}$$


* **At the bottom surface ($N_2$):** The floor supports the combined weight of both blocks.


$$N_2 = (m_1 + m_2) \cdot g$$


$$N_2 = (5 + 10) \cdot 9.81 = 147.15 \text{ N}$$

---

### 2. Calculate the Friction Forces

Kinetic friction acts at both the top and bottom of the 10 kg block, opposing its motion.

* **Friction from the top block ($f_{k1}$):** Since the 5 kg block is stationary (tied to the wall), it "rubs" against the moving 10 kg block.


$$f_{k1} = \mu_k \cdot N_1$$


$$f_{k1} = 0.2 \cdot 49.05 = 9.81 \text{ N}$$


* **Friction from the floor ($f_{k2}$):**


$$f_{k2} = \mu_k \cdot N_2$$


$$f_{k2} = 0.2 \cdot 147.15 = 29.43 \text{ N}$$

---

### 3. Apply Newton's Second Law to the 10 kg Block

The net force on the 10 kg block is the applied force minus both frictional resistances.

**The Equation of Motion:**


$$\sum F = m_2 \cdot a$$


$$F - f_{k1} - f_{k2} = m_2 \cdot a$$


**Substituting the values:**


$$45 \text{ N} - 9.81 \text{ N} - 29.43 \text{ N} = 10 \text{ kg} \cdot a$$


**Simplifying the terms:**


$$45 - 39.24 = 10 \cdot a$$


$$5.76 = 10 \cdot a$$


**Solving for $a$:**


$$a = \frac{5.76}{10}$$


$$a = 0.576 \text{ m/s}^2$$

---

### Final Result:
The acceleration of the 10 kg block is **$0.576 \text{ m/s}^2$**.
