To solve for the acceleration of the 10 kg block, we need to identify all the forces acting on it. Since the 5 kg block is tied to the wall, it remains stationary while the 10 kg block slides underneath it.

### 1. Identify the Forces and Normal Forces

First, we determine the normal forces at both contact surfaces:
* **Top surface ($N_1$):** The normal force between the 5 kg and 10 kg block is equal to the weight of the top block.
    $$N_1 = m_1 \cdot g = 5 \text{ kg} \cdot 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2 = 49.05 \text{ N}$$
* **Bottom surface ($N_2$):** The normal force between the 10 kg block and the floor is the total weight of both blocks.
    $$N_2 = (m_1 + m_2) \cdot g = (5 + 10) \text{ kg} \cdot 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2 = 147.15 \text{ N}$$



---

### 2. Calculate the Friction Forces

Kinetic friction acts at both the top and bottom surfaces of the 10 kg block, opposing its motion.

* **Friction from the top block ($f_{k1}$):**
    $$f_{k1} = \mu_k \cdot N_1 = 0.2 \cdot 49.05 \text{ N} = 9.81 \text{ N}$$
* **Friction from the floor ($f_{k2}$):**
    $$f_{k2} = \mu_k \cdot N_2 = 0.2 \cdot 147.15 \text{ N} = 29.43 \text{ N}$$

---

### 3. Apply Newton's Second Law to the 10 kg Block

The 10 kg block is being pulled by a 45 N force, but it must overcome the friction from the floor **and** the friction from the stationary block on top.

**The Equation of Motion:**
$$\sum F = m_2 \cdot a$$
$$F_{applied} - f_{k1} - f_{k2} = m_2 \cdot a$$

**Substituting the known values:**
$$45 \text{ N} - 9.81 \text{ N} - 29.43 \text{ N} = 10 \text{ kg} \cdot a$$

**Simplifying the terms:**
$$45 - 39.24 = 10 \cdot a$$
$$5.76 = 10 \cdot a$$

**Solving for $a$:**
$$a = \frac{5.76}{10}$$
$$a = 0.576 \text{ m/s}^2$$

---

### Final Result:
The acceleration of the 10 kg block is **$0.576 \text{ m/s}^2$**.

### Note on the 5 kg Block:
Because the 5 kg block is tied to the wall, its acceleration is **0**. The tension in the string holding it would be equal to the friction force $f_{k1}$ (9.81 N).
