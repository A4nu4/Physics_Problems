To find the final speed of the combined mass, we need to break this problem into two distinct parts: the **energy transformation** as the first block slides down, and the **momentum conservation** during the collision.

### Given Starting Values:
* **Mass of Block 1 ($m_1$):** $0.5 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial Height ($h$):** $3.0 \text{ m}$
* **Mass of Block 2 ($m_2$):** $1.5 \text{ kg}$ (initially at rest)
* **Acceleration due to gravity ($g$):** $9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

### Step 1: Find the speed of Block 1 at the bottom
As the block slides down the frictionless track, its gravitational potential energy ($PE$) is converted entirely into kinetic energy ($KE$).

$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$

The mass $m_1$ cancels out, so the speed at the bottom ($v_1$) depends only on the height:
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.86} \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}$$



---

### Step 2: Conservation of Momentum during the collision
The collision is **perfectly inelastic** because the blocks stick together. According to the Law of Conservation of Momentum, the total momentum before the collision must equal the total momentum after the collision.

$$\text{Momentum}_{\text{before}} = \text{Momentum}_{\text{after}}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)V_f$$

Since Block 2 is initially at rest ($v_2 = 0$), the equation simplifies to:
$$m_1v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)V_f$$



---

### Step 3: Calculate the Final Speed ($V_f$)
Rearrange the momentum equation to solve for $V_f$:
$$V_f = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1 + m_2}$$

Substitute the known values:
$$V_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.672}{0.5 + 1.5}$$
$$V_f = \frac{3.836}{2.0}$$
$$V_f = 1.918 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Final Result:
The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is approximately **$1.92 \text{ m/s}$**.
