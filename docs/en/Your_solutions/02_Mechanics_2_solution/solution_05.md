To solve this problem, we apply the principle of **Conservation of Linear Momentum**, as there are no external horizontal forces acting on the runner-cart system.

### Given Starting Values:
* **Mass of the runner ($m_1$):** $70 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial velocity of the runner ($v_1$):** $3 \text{ m/s}$
* **Mass of the cart ($m_2$):** $140 \text{ kg}$
* **Initial velocity of the cart ($v_2$):** $0 \text{ m/s}$ (stationary)

---

### 1. Calculate the Final Speed ($V_f$)
This is a **perfectly inelastic collision** because the runner and the cart move together as a single unit after the interaction. The total momentum before the jump must equal the total momentum after.

**Momentum Equation:**
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)V_f$$



Substitute the known values:
$$(70 \text{ kg} \cdot 3 \text{ m/s}) + (140 \text{ kg} \cdot 0 \text{ m/s}) = (70 \text{ kg} + 140 \text{ kg}) \cdot V_f$$
$$210 + 0 = 210 \cdot V_f$$
$$V_f = \frac{210}{210} = 1 \text{ m/s}$$

**Result:** The final speed of the cart with the runner is **$1 \text{ m/s}$**.

---

### 2. Is Kinetic Energy Conserved?
To determine if kinetic energy ($KE$) is conserved, we compare the total energy before and after the collision.

#### Initial Kinetic Energy ($KE_i$):
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2v_2^2$$
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3^2) + 0 = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \text{ J}$$

#### Final Kinetic Energy ($KE_f$):
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)V_f^2$$
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1^2) = 105 \text{ J}$$

---

### Explanation
**No, kinetic energy is not conserved.** In this collision, the kinetic energy decreased from **$315 \text{ J}$** to **$105 \text{ J}$**. This loss of **$210 \text{ J}$** occurs because this is an **inelastic collision**. The "missing" energy is converted into other forms, such as:
* **Thermal Energy (Heat):** Generated by friction between the runner's shoes and the cart.
* **Sound Energy:** The noise made when the runner lands.
* **Internal Work:** Energy spent deforming the runner's muscles or the cart's structure slightly upon impact.

While **momentum** is always conserved in a closed system, **kinetic energy** is only conserved in perfectly elastic collisions (like those between subatomic particles or ideal billiard balls).
