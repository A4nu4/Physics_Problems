To solve this problem, we will analyze the provided position equation to determine the fundamental characteristics of the oscillating mass-spring system.

### Given Starting Values:
* **Mass ($m$):** $10 \text{ kg}$
* **Position Equation:** $x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t) \text{ m}$
* From the equation's standard form $x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)$, we identify:
    * **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
    * **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** $10\pi \text{ rad/s}$

---

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### 1. Calculate the Spring Constant ($k$)
The angular frequency $\omega$ for a simple harmonic oscillator is related to the mass ($m$) and the spring constant ($k$) by the formula:
$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

To find the spring constant, rearrange the equation to solve for $k$:
$$k = m \omega^2$$

Substitute the given values:
$$k = (10 \text{ kg}) \cdot (10\pi \text{ rad/s})^2$$
$$k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$$
$$k = 1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$$

Using the approximation $\pi^2 \approx 9.8696$:
$$k \approx 9869.6 \text{ N/m}$$

**Result:** The spring constant is exactly **$1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$**, which is approximately **$9870 \text{ N/m}$**.

#### 2. Calculate the Total Mechanical Energy ($E$)
The total mechanical energy of an ideal mass-spring system is conserved and is equal to the maximum potential energy stored in the spring when it is at its maximum displacement (the amplitude):
$$E_{total} = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$$

Substitute the values for $k$ and $A$:
$$E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot (1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}) \cdot (0.2 \text{ m})^2$$
$$E = (500\pi^2) \cdot (0.04) \text{ J}$$
$$E = 20\pi^2 \text{ J}$$

Using the approximation $\pi^2 \approx 9.8696$:
$$E \approx 197.39 \text{ J}$$

---

### Final Results:
* **Spring Constant ($k$):** **$1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$** ($\approx 9870 \text{ N/m}$)
* **Total Mechanical Energy ($E$):** **$20\pi^2 \text{ J}$** ($\approx 197.4 \text{ J}$)
