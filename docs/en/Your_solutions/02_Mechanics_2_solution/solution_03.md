To find the speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom of its swing, we use the principle of **Conservation of Mechanical Energy**. The potential energy at the release point is converted into kinetic energy at the lowest point.

### Given Starting Values:
* **Length of the pendulum ($L$):** $1.0 \text{ m}$
* **Initial angle ($\theta$):** $15^\circ$
* **Acceleration due to gravity ($g$):** $9.81 \text{ m/s}^2$
* **Initial speed ($v_0$):** $0 \text{ m/s}$ (released from rest)

---

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### 1. Determine the Change in Height ($h$)
When the pendulum is at an angle $\theta$, its vertical height relative to the bottom of the swing is determined by trigonometry. The vertical distance from the pivot to the bob is $L \cos(\theta)$. Therefore, the height $h$ above the lowest point is:
$$h = L - L \cos(\theta) = L(1 - \cos(\theta))$$



Substituting the values:
$$h = 1.0 \cdot (1 - \cos(15^\circ))$$
Using $\cos(15^\circ) \approx 0.9659$:
$$h = 1.0 \cdot (1 - 0.9659) = 0.0341 \text{ m}$$

#### 2. Apply Conservation of Energy
At the release point, the energy is entirely potential ($PE$). At the bottom, the energy is entirely kinetic ($KE$):
$$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

The mass ($m$) cancels out, showing that the speed is independent of the bob's weight:
$$gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2$$
$$v = \sqrt{2gh}$$

#### 3. Calculate the Final Speed ($v$)
Substitute the values for $g$ and $h$ into the equation:
$$v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 0.0341}$$
$$v = \sqrt{0.669}$$
$$v \approx 0.818 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Final Result:
The speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom of its swing is approximately **$0.82 \text{ m/s}$**.
