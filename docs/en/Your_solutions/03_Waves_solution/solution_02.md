To find the speed of the wave on a guitar string, we analyze the relationship between the string's length, the wavelength of the fundamental frequency, and the wave speed.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Length of the string ($L$):** $64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}$
* **Fundamental Frequency ($f_1$):** $330 \text{ Hz}$
* **Harmonic:** $n = 1$ (one antinode)

---

### 1. Determine the Wavelength ($\lambda$)

For a string fixed at both ends, the fundamental frequency (the first harmonic) occurs when the length of the string is exactly half of one wavelength. This creates a single "hump" or antinode between the two fixed nodes.


$$L = \frac{\lambda}{2}$$


Rearranging to solve for the wavelength:


$$\lambda = 2L$$


Substituting the given length:


$$\lambda = 2 \cdot 0.64 \text{ m}$$


$$\lambda = 1.28 \text{ m}$$

---

### 2. Calculate the Wave Speed ($v$)

The speed of a wave is the product of its frequency and its wavelength, defined by the fundamental wave equation:


$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$


Substitute the known values for frequency and the calculated wavelength:


$$v = 330 \text{ Hz} \cdot 1.28 \text{ m}$$


$$v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Final Result:
The speed of the wave on the guitar string is **$422.4 \text{ m/s}$**.
