To find the phase difference between two points on a wave, we use the relationship between spatial separation (path difference) and the angular progression of the wave.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Separation distance ($\Delta x$):** $\frac{\lambda}{3}$
* **Wavelength:** $\lambda$

---

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### 1. The Phase-Path Relationship
In a periodic wave, a full wavelength ($\lambda$) corresponds to a complete cycle or a phase change of $2\pi$ radians. The ratio of phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) to $2\pi$ is equal to the ratio of the distance ($\Delta x$) to the wavelength ($\lambda$):


$$\frac{\Delta \phi}{2\pi} = \frac{\Delta x}{\lambda}$$

#### 2. Rearrange for Phase Difference
To solve for $\Delta \phi$, we rearrange the formula:


$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \Delta x$$

#### 3. Substitute the Given Distance
Substitute $\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}$ into the equation:


$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \left( \frac{\lambda}{3} \right)$$


The $\lambda$ terms cancel out:


$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}$$

---

### Final Result:
The phase difference between the two points is **$\frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians** (which is equivalent to $120^\circ$).
