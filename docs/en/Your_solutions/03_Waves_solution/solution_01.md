To find the wavelength of a sound wave, we use the fundamental wave equation that relates speed, frequency, and wavelength.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Frequency ($f$):** $440 \text{ Hz}$ (remains constant regardless of the medium)
* **Speed in air ($v_{air}$):** $343 \text{ m/s}$
* **Speed in water ($v_{water}$):** $1482 \text{ m/s}$

---

### The Fundamental Wave Formula:

$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

To solve for the wavelength ($\lambda$), we rearrange the formula:


$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

---

### 1. Wavelength in Air ($\lambda_{air}$)

Using the speed of sound in air:


$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$


$$\lambda_{air} \approx 0.7795 \text{ m}$$


**Result:** The wavelength in air is approximately **$0.78 \text{ m}$**.

---

### 2. Wavelength in Water ($\lambda_{water}$)

When a wave moves from one medium to another, its **frequency remains the same**, but its speed and wavelength change. Using the speed of sound in water:


$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482 \text{ m/s}}{440 \text{ Hz}}$$


$$\lambda_{water} \approx 3.368 \text{ m}$$


**Result:** The wavelength in water is approximately **$3.37 \text{ m}$**.
