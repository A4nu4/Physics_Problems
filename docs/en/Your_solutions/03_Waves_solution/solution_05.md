To find the distance to the cliff, we use the relationship between speed, distance, and time. In the case of an echo, the sound must travel to the cliff and then reflect back to the person.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Speed of sound ($v$):** $343 \text{ m/s}$
* **Total time for echo ($t_{total}$):** $1 \text{ s}$

---

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### 1. Determine the Time One-Way ($t$)
The total time of 1 second represents the "round trip" of the sound wave. To find the distance to the cliff, we only need the time it took for the sound to reach the cliff (half of the total time).


$$t = \frac{t_{total}}{2}$$


$$t = \frac{1 \text{ s}}{2} = 0.5 \text{ s}$$

---

#### 2. Apply the Distance Formula
Distance ($d$) is the product of the speed of the wave and the time it travels:


$$d = v \cdot t$$


Substitute the known values:


$$d = 343 \text{ m/s} \cdot 0.5 \text{ s}$$


$$d = 171.5 \text{ m}$$

---

### Final Result:
The cliff is **$171.5 \text{ m}$** away.
