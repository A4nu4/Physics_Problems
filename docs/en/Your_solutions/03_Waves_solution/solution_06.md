To analyze the given wave equation, we compare it to the standard form of a traveling harmonic wave.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Wave Equation:** $y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$
* **Standard Form:** $y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$

From this comparison, we identify the following parameters:
* **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.05 \text{ m}$
* **Wave Number ($k$):** $2\pi \text{ rad/m}$
* **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** $50\pi \text{ rad/s}$

---

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### a) Amplitude ($A$)
The amplitude is the maximum displacement from the equilibrium position, found directly from the coefficient of the sine function.


**Result:** $A = 0.05 \text{ m}$

---

#### b) Wavelength ($\lambda$)
The wave number $k$ is related to the wavelength by the formula:


$$k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$$


Rearranging to solve for $\lambda$:


$$\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k}$$


$$\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi}$$


**Result:** $\lambda = 1 \text{ m}$

---

#### c) Frequency ($f$)
The angular frequency $\omega$ is related to the linear frequency by the formula:


$$\omega = 2\pi f$$


Rearranging to solve for $f$:


$$f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}$$


$$f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi}$$


**Result:** $f = 25 \text{ Hz}$

---

#### d) Wave Speed ($v$)
The speed of the wave can be calculated using the relationship between wavelength and frequency:


$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$


$$v = 25 \text{ Hz} \cdot 1 \text{ m}$$


Alternatively, using the ratio of angular frequency to wave number:


$$v = \frac{\omega}{k} = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ m/s}$$


**Result:** $v = 25 \text{ m/s}$

---

### Final Summary:
* **Amplitude:** $0.05 \text{ m}$
* **Wavelength:** $1 \text{ m}$
* **Frequency:** $25 \text{ Hz}$
* **Wave Speed:** $25 \text{ m/s}$
