To find the wavelength of a standing wave on a string, we examine the relationship between the string's length and the number of nodes or antinodes present.



### Given Starting Values:
* **Length of the string ($L$):** $80 \text{ cm} = 0.8 \text{ m}$
* **Number of antinodes ($n$):** $4$ (This corresponds to the 4th harmonic)

---

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### 1. Understand the Harmonic Relationship
For a string fixed at both ends, each "loop" or antinode represents half of a wavelength ($\lambda/2$). If there are $n$ antinodes, the total length of the string $L$ is equal to $n$ half-wavelengths:


$$L = n \cdot \frac{\lambda}{2}$$

#### 2. Rearrange to Solve for Wavelength ($\lambda$)
To find the wavelength, we rearrange the formula:


$$\lambda = \frac{2L}{n}$$

#### 3. Substitute the Known Values
Substitute $L = 0.8 \text{ m}$ and $n = 4$ into the equation:


$$\lambda = \frac{2 \cdot 0.8 \text{ m}}{4}$$


$$\lambda = \frac{1.6 \text{ m}}{4}$$


$$\lambda = 0.4 \text{ m}$$

---

### Final Result:
The wavelength of the wave is **$0.4 \text{ m}$** (or **$40 \text{ cm}$**).
