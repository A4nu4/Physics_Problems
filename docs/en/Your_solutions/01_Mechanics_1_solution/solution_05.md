To solve this relative velocity problem, we must ensure the boat's motion relative to the riverbank is directed purely North. This means the boat must head at an angle upstream (West) to counteract the river's flow.

### Given Values:

* **Velocity of the river ($v_r$):** $2 \text{ m/s}$ [East]
* **Speed of the boat in still water ($v_{bw}$):** $5 \text{ m/s}$
* **Width of the river ($d$):** $200 \text{ meters}$
* **Desired direction relative to the ground:** North

---

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### 1. Determine the Direction (Heading Angle)

To travel directly North, the eastward velocity of the river must be exactly cancelled out by the westward component of the boat's velocity relative to the water.

Let $\theta$ be the angle at which the boat heads, measured **West of North**.

In the velocity vector triangle:

* The hypotenuse is the boat's speed relative to the water ($v_{bw} = 5 \text{ m/s}$).
* The opposite side is the river's speed ($v_r = 2 \text{ m/s}$).

Using the sine function:


$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{v_r}{v_{bw}}$$

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$

$$\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58^\circ$$

**Result:** The boat should head at an angle of approximately **$23.58^\circ$ West of North** (upstream).

#### 2. Calculate the Resultant Velocity Across the River

The actual speed of the boat relative to the ground ($v_b$) is the northward component of the boat's velocity. We can find this using the Pythagorean theorem since the vectors form a right-angled triangle:

$$v_b^2 + v_r^2 = v_{bw}^2$$

$$v_b = \sqrt{v_{bw}^2 - v_r^2}$$

$$v_b = \sqrt{5^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{25 - 4}$$

$$v_b = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}$$

#### 3. Calculate the Time to Cross

The time taken to cross the river depends only on the velocity component perpendicular to the banks (the Northward velocity).

$$\text{Time} (t) = \frac{\text{Width of river}}{\text{Velocity North}}$$

$$t = \frac{200 \text{ m}}{\sqrt{21} \text{ m/s}}$$

$$t \approx \frac{200}{4.5826} \approx 43.64 \text{ seconds}$$

---

### Final Answer:

* **Direction:** The boat should head **$23.58^\circ$ West of North**.
* **Time:** It will take approximately **$43.64$ seconds** to cross the river.
