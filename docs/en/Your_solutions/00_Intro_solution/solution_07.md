This is a classic puzzle that can be solved simply by focusing on the total time elapsed rather than the infinite series of the fly's individual trips.

### Task Conditions and Values

* **Distance to wall ($d$):** $10\text{ m}$
* **Bicycle speed ($v_b$):** $1\text{ m/s}$
* **Fly speed ($v_f$):** $2\text{ m/s}$
* **Goal:** Calculate the total distance traveled by the fly until the bicycle reaches the wall.

---

### Step-by-Step Solution

**Step 1: Determine how long the bicycle is in motion**
Since the bicycle moves at a constant speed, we can find the time ($t$) it takes to reach the wall:


$$t = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}} = \frac{10\text{ m}}{1\text{ m/s}} = 10\text{ seconds}$$

**Step 2: Determine how long the fly is flying**
The fly is in the air for the exact same amount of time as the bicycle is moving. Once the bicycle hits the wall at $t = 10\text{ s}$, the fly's journey ends.


$$\text{Time for fly } (t_f) = 10\text{ seconds}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the total distance the fly travels**
Since the fly flies at a constant speed of $2\text{ m/s}$ for the entire duration of 10 seconds, we simply multiply its speed by the total time:


$$\text{Distance} = \text{speed} \times \text{time}$$

$$\text{Distance} = 2\text{ m/s} \times 10\text{ s}$$

$$\text{Distance} = 20\text{ meters}$$

---

### Final Answer

The fly travels a total distance of **$20\text{ meters}$** before being crushed.
