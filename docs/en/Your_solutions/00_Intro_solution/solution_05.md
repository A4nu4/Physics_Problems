To find the components of a vector, we use trigonometric ratios to project the vector's magnitude onto the horizontal ($x$) and vertical ($y$) axes.

### Task Conditions and Values

* **Vector Magnitude ($A$):** $15$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $60^\circ$ (measured from the horizontal axis)
* **Goal:** Calculate the horizontal component ($A_x$) and the vertical component ($A_y$).

---

### Step-by-Step Solution

**Step 1: Calculate the horizontal component ($A_x$)**
The horizontal component is the side adjacent to the angle. We use the cosine function:


$$A_x = A \cos(\theta)$$

$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$


Since $\cos(60^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}$:


$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5$$

**Step 2: Calculate the vertical component ($A_y$)**
The vertical component is the side opposite the angle. We use the sine function:


$$A_y = A \sin(\theta)$$

$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$


Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$:


$$A_y = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$$

$$A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99$$

---

### Final Answer

The components of vector $\vec{A}$ are:

* **Horizontal component ($A_x$):** $7.5$
* **Vertical component ($A_y$):** $\frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}$ or **$12.99$**
