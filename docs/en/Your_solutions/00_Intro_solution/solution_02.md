To solve a system of linear equations, we look for the specific point $(x, y)$ where the two lines intersect.

### Task Conditions and Values

* **Equation 1:** $2x + 3y = 12$
* **Equation 2:** $x - y = 1$
* **Goal:** Find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy both equations simultaneously.

---

### Step-by-Step Solution

We will use the **Substitution Method** to solve this system.

**Step 1: Isolate one variable**
From **Equation 2**, it is easy to express $x$ in terms of $y$:


$$x = y + 1$$

**Step 2: Substitute into the other equation**
Substitute $(y + 1)$ for $x$ in **Equation 1**:


$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$

**Step 3: Solve for $y$**
Distribute the $2$ and combine like terms:


$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$

$$5y + 2 = 12$$


Subtract $2$ from both sides:


$$5y = 10$$


Divide by $5$:


$$y = 2$$

**Step 4: Solve for $x$**
Now, plug the value of $y$ back into our isolated equation from Step 1:


$$x = 2 + 1$$

$$x = 3$$

---

### Final Answer

The solution to the system of equations is:
**$x = 3$**
**$y = 2$**

(Verification: $2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12$, and $3 - 2 = 1$. Both conditions are satisfied.)
