To find the area under the curve of the sine function without using graphical representation, we perform the definite integral calculation over the given interval.


### Task Conditions and Values

* **Function:** $f(x) = \sin(x)$
* **Interval:** from $x = 0$ to $x = \pi$
* **Goal:** Calculate the definite integral to find the area.

---

### Step-by-Step Solution

**Step 1: Set up the definite integral**
The area $A$ is defined by the integral of the function between the lower and upper limits:


$$A = \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) dx$$

**Step 2: Find the antiderivative**
The antiderivative of $\sin(x)$ is $-\cos(x)$. According to the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:


$$A = \left[ -\cos(x) \right]_{0}^{\pi}$$

**Step 3: Evaluate at the boundaries**
Substitute the upper limit ($\pi$) and subtract the value of the antiderivative at the lower limit ($0$):


$$A = (-\cos(\pi)) - (-\cos(0))$$

**Step 4: Substitute the trigonometric values**
Recall that $\cos(\pi) = -1$ and $\cos(0) = 1$:


$$A = -(-1) - (-1)$$

$$A = 1 + 1$$

$$A = 2$$

---

### Final Answer

The area under the curve of $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $x = 0$ to $x = \pi$ is exactly **$2$**.
