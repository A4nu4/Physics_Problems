To find the average current of the lightning bolt, we use the definition of current as the rate of flow of charge over a specific interval of time.

### 1. Identify the Given Values

* **Charge ($Q$):** $30 \text{ C}$
* **Time ($t$):** $2 \text{ ms} = 2 \times 10^{-3} \text{ s}$ (or $0.002 \text{ s}$)

---

### 2. Apply the Current Formula

The average current ($I_{avg}$) is defined by the formula:


$$I_{avg} = \frac{Q}{t}$$

Substituting the values into the equation:


$$I_{avg} = \frac{30 \text{ C}}{0.002 \text{ s}}$$

---

### 3. Final Result

$$I_{avg} = 15,000 \text{ A}$$

The average current of the lightning bolt is **$15,000 \text{ A}$** (or $15 \text{ kA}$).

For perspective, a typical household circuit breaker is rated for about **15 A** to **20 A**, meaning this single lightning bolt carries roughly **1,000 times** the current capacity of an entire home's electrical panel.
