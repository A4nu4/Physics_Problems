To calculate the total charge and energy for capacitors in parallel, we use the equivalent capacitance and the standard formulas for charge and stored potential energy.

### 1. Equivalent Capacitance ($C_{eq}$)

For capacitors in parallel, the total capacitance is the sum of the individual capacitances.


$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2$$

$$C_{eq} = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F} = 10\,\mu\text{F}$$

---

### 2. Total Charge Stored ($Q_{total}$)

The total charge stored by the combination is determined by the relation $Q = CV$. In a parallel circuit, the voltage across each capacitor is equal to the battery voltage ($10\text{ V}$).


$$Q_{total} = C_{eq} \times V$$

$$Q_{total} = (10 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}) \times 10\text{ V} = 100 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}$$


The total charge is **$100\,\mu\text{C}$**.

---

### 3. Total Energy Stored ($U_{total}$)

The energy $U$ stored in the electric field of the capacitors is calculated using the formula $U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2$.


$$U_{total} = \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} V^2$$

$$U_{total} = \frac{1}{2} (10 \times 10^{-6}\text{ F}) (10\text{ V})^2$$

$$U_{total} = 0.5 \times (10 \times 10^{-6}) \times 100$$

$$U_{total} = 5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ J}$$


The total energy stored is **$0.5\text{ mJ}$** (millijoules).

---
