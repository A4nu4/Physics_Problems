To find the current at a specific moment in time, we use the definition of instantaneous current, which is the time rate of change of charge.

### 1. Identify the Relationship

The current $I(t)$ is the derivative of the charge function $Q(t)$ with respect to time:


$$I(t) = \frac{dQ(t)}{dt}$$

---

### 2. Differentiate the Function

Given the function $Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$:

* The derivative of $5t^2$ is $10t$.
* The derivative of the constant $5$ is $0$.

So, the current function is:


$$I(t) = 10t$$

---

### 3. Calculate the Current at $t = 3 \text{ s}$

Substitute $t = 3$ into the derived current equation:


$$I(3) = 10(3)$$

$$I(3) = 30 \text{ A}$$

The current flowing through the wire at $t = 3 \text{ s}$ is **$30 \text{ A}$**.
